About us

In our studio you can choose the direction of yoga that suits you. We have various types of yoga: ashtanga yoga, universal yoga, Iyengar yoga, hatha yoga, Himalayan yoga, kundalini, vajra, classical, vinyasa flow, children's yoga and lunch yoga.

All the teachers working in our studio are certified yoga instructors with a huge amount of knowledge that they are all ready to share and discover something new for you. Teachers regularly improve both their own level of practice and the quality of teaching by attending various seminars and courses.

Classes
Hatha yoga

Hatha yoga extends well beyond being a sophisticated physical exercise system and integrates ideas of ethics, diet, cleansing, pranayama (breathing exercises), meditation and a system for spiritual development of the yogi.

Ashtanga vinyasa yoga

Ashtanga vinyasa yoga emphasizes certain main components, namely tristhana ("three places of action or attention", or the more physical aspects of poses) and vinyasa (which Sharath Jois defines as a system of breathing and movement)

Kundalini yoga

The practice of kriyas and meditations in Kundalini Yoga are designed to raise complete body awareness to prepare the body, nervous system, and mind to handle the energy of Kundalini rising.

Bikram yoga

Bikram Yoga Beginning Series classes run for 90 minutes and always consist of 26 postures, namely 24 asanas, one pranayama (breathing exercise), and one shatkarma (a purification) in a fixed sequence.

Our team

My teachings are grounded in the traditional lineage of Hatha yoga and the deep philosophy of Tantric practices married with the movement of Vinyasa Flow, the precision of Anusara, the dance of Rasa Yoga – one of the most heartfelt synthesis of yoga, and the energetics of ParaYoga.

I hope for each student to experience a journey that goes beyond the body and pierces deeply into the heart by weaving together asana, mantra, mudra, pranayama, meditation and chanting, along with shadow work, ritual, and a deep connection with nature.
